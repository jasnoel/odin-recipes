Simple Sauteed Sesame Chicken

A healthier, simpler, and more savory take on sesame chicken. This recipe can be made with regular flour or with a gluten-free flour mix, along with basic ingredients you can already find in your kitchen. My husband absolutely loves it and it is the perfect alternative to a night in with take-out. Best served with steamed rice, broccoli, carrots, and water chestnuts. Serve with steamed rice and vegetables.

  • 4 large chicken breast halves
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 2 tablespoons all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon red pepper flakes
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • Â½ teaspoon onion powder
  • 3 tablespoons low-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil
  • 2 teaspoons honey (Optional)
  • 1 tablespoon
  • olive oil
  1. Cut chicken breasts into 1 1/2-inch slices about 1/4-inch thick. Season with salt and pepper. Combine flour, red pepper flakes, garlic powder, and onion powder in a large bowl. Add chicken and toss well. Add soy sauce, sesame oil, and honey; stir to coat.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add chicken and cook, stirring occasionally, until golden brown and juices run clear, 10 to 15 minutes.
